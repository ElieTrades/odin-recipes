What's Kimchi?

Kimchi is a beloved staple of Korean cuisine, known for its bold flavors, vibrant color, and deep cultural roots. This traditional dish is made by fermenting vegetables -most commonly napa cabbage- with a flavorful blend of garlic, ginger, chili pepper, fish sauce, and other seasonings. The result is a tangy, spicy, and slightly sour profile that develops more complexity over time as it ferments.

Beyond its taste, Kimchi is celebrated for its nutritional benefits. It is rich in probiotics, which support gut health, and packed with vitamins and antioxidants from its fresh ingredients. Whether served as a side dish, added to soups and stews, or used as a topping for rice and noodles, kimchi brings a distinctive depth of flavor that elevates even the simplest meals.

What's in it?

  • 1 large napa cabbage (cut into quarters)
  • 1/4 cup salt
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 5-6 cloves garlic
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (or soy sauce for a vegetarian option)
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru), depending on spice preference
  • 4 green onions (chopped)
  • 1 small carrot
  • 1 small daikon radish
  • Optional: 1 small pear or apple

How To Make Kimchi

  1. Cut the napa cabbage into quarters and rinse it thoroughly. Soak it in salted water for several hours until softened.
  2. Rinse the cabbage to remove excess salt and drain well.
  3. In a bowl, mix garlic, ginger, sugar, fish sauce, and Korean red pepper flakes to form a paste.
  4. Add chopped green onions, carrot, and radish to the paste and mix it thoroughly.
  5. Rub the seasoning mixture evenly onto the cabbage leaves (you can use gloves).
  6. Pack the coated cabbage tightly into a clean jar or container.
  7. Leave it at room temperature for 1–2 days to ferment, then transfer to the refrigerator.
  8. Let it continue fermenting for several days before eating for the best flavor.
